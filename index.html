architectureThree-layer observability

KleinProbe sits between the device and your experiment, projecting global calibration data onto the specific physical qubits your circuit actually uses.

LAYER 1
backend.properties()
Global device state. All 156 qubits. Updated ~daily. Tells you nothing about which qubits your circuit uses.
IBM provides
LAYER 2
KleinProbe
Circuit-conditioned snapshot. Projects calibration onto your layout. Measures the qubits that matter for your experiment.
this module
LAYER 3
Your experiment
Actual results. Now you know which jobs ran during a noisy period and which ran on a healthy chip.
you run

The probe circuit is an 18-qubit Klein bottle stabilizer code — a non-orientable topological circuit whose syndrome output is analytically predictable. The topology tells you what should happen; the hardware tells you what does happen; the gap characterises the noise.

usageSingle snapshot

Run a probe and get a noise snapshot in one call.

from qiskit_ibm_runtime import QiskitRuntimeService
from kleinprobe import KleinProbe

service = QiskitRuntimeService()
backend = service.backend("ibm_marrakesh")

probe = KleinProbe(backend)
snap = probe.run()
print(snap.report())

usageDrift tracking

Monitor calibration drift across a long experiment. Each checkpoint adds ~15 seconds and runs as a PUB alongside your main job — zero additional queue wait time.

tracker = probe.track()

tracker.checkpoint("before")
run_my_experiment_batch_1()
tracker.checkpoint("mid")
run_my_experiment_batch_2()
tracker.checkpoint("after")

print(tracker.report())

metricsWhat KleinProbe measures

Four circuit-conditioned noise metrics, each sensitive to a different aspect of hardware behaviour.

metricmeaningsensitive to
H Shannon entropy of syndrome distribution (bits) Total noise — more noise = higher H. Increases with T2 decay and gate errors.
inv Klein invariant fraction — P(syndrome bit 0 = 1) Quality of the specific qubit hosting the antipodal edge. Sensitive to RO and T2 of that qubit.
f Dominant pattern frequency Topological SNR — how strongly the hardware reproduces the predicted syndrome.
Z Z-score vs flat baseline (1/64) Overall circuit health. Healthy chip: Z > 100σ. Z < 50σ signals routing or calibration problems.

All metrics are circuit-conditioned: they reflect the physical qubits your circuit uses, identified from the transpiler layout automatically. Not the device average.
